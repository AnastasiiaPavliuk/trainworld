Yet again, a compromise was reached.

In Flanders, with the exception of the Antwerp - Dutch border section, no high-speed train lines would be constructed. However, lines would be adapted in a manner that benefited both the domestic and high-speed trains.

But the works had to begin at the same time in both the North and the South.

This simultaneous construction was part of Wallonia's intention to make sure the HSL between Leuven and Liège would not be put on the back burner.

The new face of the Belgian railways

NMBS/SNCB Split

To remain in line with European regulations, the NMBS/SNCB split into 3 companies on January 1, 2005.

SNCB-Holding

Coordinator

SNCB

Railway operator

  • Manages and modernises the Belgian railway
  • Maintains the rolling stock
  • Manages all the stations and stops

Infrabel

infrastructure manager

  • Operates the passenger trains
  • Infrastructure and controls the train traffic safety

In 2014,NMBS/SNCB and Infrabel became two independent companies, with the responsibilities of SNCB-Holding shared out between both.

Investment and modernisation

The NMBS/SNCB and Infrabel are actively investing in the 21st-century modernization of the railway network. In 2011, the introduction of 305 lightweight Desiro Main Line electric units improved local and regional passenger transport.

Desiro Main Line was supremely good for suburb routes with many stops.
